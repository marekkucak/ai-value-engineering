## Part 2 — Dimension Calibration

### Value (1–10): EUR annual saving if implemented

| Rating | Annual Saving | Examples |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| 9–10 | >EUR 40k | Eliminate 5+ FTE friction; Release >20 days cash; Consolidate 3+ SaaS |
| 7–8 | EUR 20k–40k | Eliminate 2–3 FTE friction; Release 10–20 days cash |
| 5–6 | EUR 10k–20k | Eliminate 1 FTE friction; Release 5–10 days cash |
| 3–4 | EUR 3k–10k | Minor friction reduction; Small SaaS saving |
| 1–2 | <EUR 3k | Negligible impact |

### Frequency (1–10): How often does the workflow run?

| Rating | Frequency | Examples |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| 9–10 | >5/day | Invoice triage, support tickets, AR aging |
| 7–8 | Daily | Lead qualification, data reconciliation |
| 5–6 | Weekly | Mgmt reporting, contract extraction |
| 3–4 | Monthly | Consolidation reviews, forecasting |
| 1–2 | <Monthly | Annual planning, one-off migrations |

### Reviewability (1–10): How easy to verify AI output?

| Rating | Criteria | Examples |
|--------|----------|----------|
| 9–10 | Binary check (yes/no), clear audit trail | AR aging flags, SaaS deprecation alerts |
| 7–8 | Spot-check 10%, clear approval workflow | Invoice routing, support triage |
| 5–6 | Domain expert review required | Contract extraction, compliance flags |
| 3–4 | Complex judgment, multiple reviewers | Data reconciliation, forecasting |
| 1–2 | Cannot easily verify correctness | Novel generative content |

### Data Availability (1–10): Is structured data available?

| Rating | Data Source | Examples |
|--------|------------|----------|
| 9–10 | Clean ERP export, REST API, SSO logs | Workday, NetSuite, Datadog APIs |
| 7–8 | Mostly clean, occasional gaps | Email headers, PDF invoices with OCR |
| 5–6 | Mixed structured/unstructured | Email + attachments, Jira + notes |
| 3–4 | Mostly manual, sparse exports | Spreadsheets, manual process logs |
| 1–2 | Unstructured, requires heavy ETL | Handwritten notes, video transcripts |

### Risk (1–10): What goes wrong if AI makes an error?

| Rating | Impact | Examples |
|--------|--------|----------|
| 9–10 | Regulatory breach, financial loss, churn | AR aging (payment delays), compliance (fines) |
| 7–8 | Financial loss, customer complaint | Invoice routing (delays), support (escalation) |
| 5–6 | Wasted time, rework required | Data reconciliation, reporting corrections |
| 3–4 | Annoyance, must be caught in review | Email draft, calendar suggestions |
| 1–2 | No material impact | Low-stakes internal metrics |

### Integration Complexity (1–10): How hard to wire into existing systems?

| Rating | Complexity | Examples |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| 9–10 | Monolithic legacy ERP, no API, custom schema | Old SAP, paper process logging |
| 7–8 | Custom ERP or API with OAuth, SOAP, SDK | Oracle, custom-built apps |
| 5–6 | REST API with some rate limits | Salesforce, HubSpot, Workday |
| 3–4 | REST API, webhooks, well-documented | Slack, modern microservices |
| 1–2 | Trivial integration, public data | Public APIs, CSV export |

### Change Resistance (1–10): How much pushback from users?

| Rating | Stakeholders | Typical Pushback |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|
| 9–10 | Senior executives, regulated roles, union | CFO, compliance officer, legal team |
| 7–8 | Department head, 20+ affected staff | VP Finance, Head of HR |
| 5–6 | Team lead, 5–10 affected staff | Finance Manager, Support Team Lead |
| 3–4 | Few affected staff, tech-savvy | Data analyst, engineer |
| 1–2 | Bot enthusiasts, no headcount impact | IT staff, metrics team |
